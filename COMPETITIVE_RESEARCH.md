### Messenger table-stakes
1. **Free** core messenger — every serious privacy rival is free; charging for basic secure messaging is itself a downgrade signal.
2. **E2E ON BY DEFAULT for 1:1 AND group** — Signal/WhatsApp/Session/Matrix-rooms; opt-in (Telegram) is the rejected anti-pattern.
3. **Forward secrecy via a ratchet** — Signal/WhatsApp set the bar; THE marker of a "real" secure messenger. Even Session lacks it in V1; Matrix group FS is only partial. **Its absence (our current state) is a clear downgrade vs Signal/WhatsApp.**
4. **Post-compromise / break-in recovery** — Signal Double Ratchet, Matrix Olm.
5. **Disappearing messages** with configurable timer — Signal, WhatsApp (24h/7d/90d), Session, Telegram Secret Chats.
6. **Contact/key verification UX** — safety-number/QR/emoji-SAS, ideally backed by key transparency for auto-verify (WhatsApp+Cloudflare 2025, Signal AKV).
7. **Multi-device with encrypted history sync** — Signal (5 + 2025 history sync), WhatsApp companion, Matrix key backup.
8. **E2E voice/video** — all offer it; tolerable as fast-follow.
9. **Group E2E that scales** — Signal/WhatsApp clean; Session/Matrix caveats >~30.
10. **Auditable open protocol / whitepaper** — Signal/Matrix/Session published; closed custom crypto (Telegram MTProto) distrusted.

## 4. WHITE-SPACE ASSESSMENT — the honest read on our wedge

**WHERE THE HYPOTHESIS HOLDS (genuinely uncontested):**
- No privacy ecosystem combines **categorized personal-finance budgeting + E2E messaging under one ordinary login**. Proton — the gold standard — has *neither* a chat product nor a budgeting tool. The pure-budget set (Budgero, uFincs, Actual, YNAB, Monarch, Copilot) has *zero* messaging. The messengers (Signal/Session/Matrix/WhatsApp) touch *no* money.

**WHERE THE WEDGE IS NOW CONTESTED — and where rivals already do pieces we cannot claim as novel:**
- **The combined wedge is no longer uncontested by ONE serious entrant: World App** (launched 2025-12-11) genuinely unifies E2E chat + money + one identity, and on **metadata it is ahead of us**. BUT its "money" is a crypto wallet, **not budgeting**; its identity is an **in-person iris scan** (regulator-banned in 5+ jurisdictions); and it has **no independent audit**. **XChat** has the same ambition with researcher-confirmed broken crypto.
- **Hosted, zero-knowledge budgeting is NOT novel:** **Budgero ($7.99/mo) already ships our exact budget-side pitch** ("client-side AES-256-GCM, we cannot read your budget"). **Actual** (free, OSS) offers optional server-unreadable E2E; **uFincs** ships E2E budgeting; **Firefly** gives full self-host isolation. So **"first/only E2E budget app" is FALSE** — we must lead with the *combination*, not the budget-E2E claim alone.
- **Default E2E, no-PII signup + metadata protection, disappearing messages, QR/safety-number + key transparency, multi-device history sync, post-quantum FS** are all already done by Signal/WhatsApp/Session — none are ours to claim as novel.

**OUR DEFENSIBLE, SHARPENED SLICE (what NO incumbent occupies):**
> **Private *budgeting* (categorized personal finance, client-side import, no Plaid/aggregation) + audited-grade E2E *messaging*, under ONE ordinary email/password identity — no iris scan, no crypto-wallet baggage, no biometric.** World's money is a wallet not a budget; Proton has no chat and no budget; Budgero/Actual have no messaging; XChat's crypto is broken. That exact intersection is empty.

**NON-NEGOTIABLES BEFORE THE PITCH IS EVEN TRUE (these gate marketing):**
1. **Budget data is PLAINTEXT server-side today** (PLATFORM.md gap #6). Until E2E ships, we are *strictly worse* than YNAB's "we never use your data" and far behind Budgero/Actual/uFincs. This is the single most important fix — it is the whole differentiator. uFincs/Budgero prove "we can't read it even if we wanted to" is a credible, shipping line.
2. **No forward secrecy in the messenger** (PLATFORM.md gap #1) — strictly behind Signal/WhatsApp and even behind World's marketing; XChat's lack of FS got it publicly mocked. Shipping true FS would also put us *ahead* of Session V1 (no FS) and Matrix group/Megolm (partial FS) — but only after it ships.
3. **Auto-adopting TOFU** (gap #3) is below baseline vs Signal's fail-closed key-change flag.
4. **Metadata** (gap #4): our shared Supabase backend leaks social graph + timing. Session (onion-routed) and World (claims no metadata) beat us here. **Be honest** — compete on integration, not metadata supremacy.

**STRUCTURAL TRADE-OFF WE INHERIT:** every private budget rival (Budgero, Actual, Firefly) **gives up automatic bank sync**, because routing bank data through the vendor breaks zero-knowledge. `money_tracker` hits the same wall: either (a) manual/CSV-first like Budgero/Firefly, or (b) **client-side aggregation** (Plaid token + decryption only on the user's device) — which, if achieved, would make us **the only private app that keeps auto-sync**. This is our single most important product decision and should be stated explicitly.

**PRICING ANCHORS:** private cohort anchors **FREE** (Actual/Firefly OSS) to **~$8/mo** (Budgero); mainstream anchors **$95–110/yr** (Copilot/Monarch/YNAB). Our combined budget+messenger bundle is the value justification for landing near the mainstream tier rather than the $8 privacy floor — but only once both halves are actually E2E.
